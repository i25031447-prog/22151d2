Cultural Traditions

Yum Cha — Morning Tea Ritual

Born in Guangzhou, Yum Cha (literally "drink tea") is the city's defining social tradition. Locals spend mornings at teahouses selecting dim sum from bamboo steamers, pouring tea for elders first as a sign of respect.

Spring Festival Flower Market

"No flower market, no New Year" — Guangzhou's iconic Xinghua Street tradition dates back to the Ming Dynasty, where millions stroll through dazzling flower markets to buy blossoms for good fortune.

Cantonese Opera

Guangzhou is the cradle of Cantonese Opera (Yueju), a UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage blending singing, martial arts, and acrobatics with unique Cantonese vocal techniques.

Guangzhou Awakened Lion Dance

The Southern Lion Dance (Xingshi) originated in Guangzhou. The famous "pole-jumping" lion dance on plum blossom poles expresses joy, anger, and curiosity through the lion's movable features.

Dragon Boat "Jing" — River Visiting

Unique to Guangzhou, villages along the Pearl River send decorated dragon boats to visit each other during Duanwu Festival, hosting feasts and celebrating community bonds.

Cantonese Storytelling — Jianggu

Jianggu is a traditional Cantonese oral storytelling art performed in teahouses and parks, narrating tales of heroes and legends in rhythmic Cantonese with wooden clappers and folding fans.
