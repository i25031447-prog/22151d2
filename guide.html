Your Complete Guangzhou Travel Guide

Recommended Itineraries

1

One-Day City Highlights

Start with dim sum breakfast at Panxi Restaurant, then visit Chen Clan Ancestral Hall. Explore Shamian Island in the afternoon, stroll along Beijing Road for shopping, and end the day with a Pearl River night cruise. A perfect introduction to Guangzhou's essence in a single day.

2

Three-Day Cultural Deep Dive

Day 1: Downtown landmarks — Canton Tower, Guangzhou Museum, Sun Yat-sen Memorial Hall. Day 2: Culture — Chen Clan Ancestral Hall, Xiguan Old Town, Cantonese Opera performance. Day 3: Nature — Baiyun Mountain hiking, Yuexiu Park, and a food tour through Shangxiajiu Street.

3

Food Lover's Journey

Dedicate each day to a different culinary experience: traditional dim sum at historic teahouses, street food exploration in Dongshankou, fresh seafood at Qingping Market, and a fine dining experience at one of Guangzhou's Michelin-starred restaurants. Include a Cantonese cooking class for a hands-on experience.

4

Family-Friendly Adventure

Perfect for families: visit Guangzhou Chimelong Paradise and Chimelong Safari Park, explore the Guangdong Science Center, ride the Canton Tower Ferris wheel, enjoy a picnic in Yuexiu Park, and have fun at the Guangzhou Zoo. Kids will love every moment!

5

Budget Traveler's Guide

Experience Guangzhou without breaking the bank: stay in budget hostels near metro stations, eat at local canteens and street stalls (meals from 15 CNY), visit free attractions like Shamian Island and temple grounds, use public transport, and explore free parks. Surprisingly affordable for international travelers.

Practical Tips

01

Getting There

Baiyun International Airport connects to 200+ cities worldwide. The Airport Express Metro Line 3 reaches downtown in 40 minutes. High-speed rail links Guangzhou to Shenzhen (30 min), Hong Kong (1 hr), and Beijing (8 hrs).

02

Getting Around

The metro system (16 lines) is the best way to explore. Get a Yang Cheng Tong transit card. Buses cost 2 CNY. Taxis start at 12 CNY. Download DiDi for ride-hailing. Biking along the Pearl River greenway is a great option.

03

Accommodation

Tianhe for modern hotels and shopping. Yuexiu for central location near cultural sites. Liwan for old-town atmosphere. Budget options near any metro station. International chains and boutique hotels are plentiful.

04

Budget

Daily budget: 200-500 CNY for mid-range travel. Street food meals 15-30 CNY. Dim sum 50-150 CNY. Metro rides 2-10 CNY. Most attractions 10-150 CNY. Guangzhou offers excellent value compared to Beijing or Shanghai.

05

Best Season

October to December for cool, dry weather. Spring (March-April) for flowers but expect rain. Summer is hot and humid (June-August). Winter is mild (10-20 deg C). Avoid national holidays unless you enjoy crowds.

06

Essential Apps

Download Alipay and WeChat (for payments), DiDi (ride-hailing), Baidu Maps or Apple Maps (navigation), and Google Translate. Set up Alipay with a foreign credit card — it works everywhere in Guangzhou.
