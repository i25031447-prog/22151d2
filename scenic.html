Explore Guangzhou's Stunning Landscapes

Must-Visit Scenic Spots

Canton Tower

The iconic 600-meter-tall Canton Tower dominates Guangzhou's skyline with its slender, twisting form. Visit the observation deck at 488 meters for stunning views, ride the world's highest Ferris wheel, or enjoy the spectacular LED light show after dark.

Yuexiu Park

The largest park in downtown Guangzhou, home to the famous Five Rams Sculpture. The park features lush gardens, the historic Zhenhai Tower, an artificial lake, and the beautiful Guangzhou Museum within its scenic grounds.

Baiyun Mountain

Known as the "Green Lung" of Guangzhou, Baiyun Mountain offers miles of hiking trails through verdant forests. At the summit, enjoy panoramic views of the entire city including waterfalls, gardens, and the serene Mingchun Valley.

Shamian Island

A small island on the Pearl River, Shamian is a preserved enclave of European colonial architecture from the 19th century. Tree-lined boulevards, historic churches, and charming cafes create a picture-perfect setting for a leisurely afternoon walk.

Sun Yat-sen Memorial Hall

A magnificent octagonal building surrounded by gardens, this memorial hall honors Dr. Sun Yat-sen, the father of modern China. Built in 1931, its striking architecture combines traditional Chinese elements with modern design. The hall hosts regular cultural performances and serves as a gathering place for local celebrations.

Pearl River Night Cruise

A cruise along the Pearl River reveals Guangzhou's dazzling nightscape. Pass under illuminated bridges, admire the glowing Canton Tower, and enjoy the city's vibrant waterfront. The cruise route passes by dozens of landmarks, making it the perfect way to unwind after a day of sightseeing while seeing the city from a completely new perspective.
